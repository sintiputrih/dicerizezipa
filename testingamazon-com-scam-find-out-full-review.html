Testingamazon.Com Scam; Find Out Full Review

In the world of business, the domain investment industry has been thriving. With the uncertainty in the stock market and cryptocurrency, hundreds of firms in the United States are offering services to enhance domain scores and online sales.

However, some of these domains may not be legitimate and may target scamming their customers. One such domain that has raised suspicion is Testingamazon.com. Let’s explore the legitimacy, features, and reviews of Testingamazon.com Scam.

 Testingamazon.com

Testingamazon.com was registered under the business offering – GIFT CARDS of reputed brands. However, it has not commenced operations to date.

There were rumors that Testingamazon would offer Amazon products for testing, but this is just speculation. Its IP, 35.202.21.90, is related to three other domains handled by Leadpages, a service provider that helps startups to boost sales.

While 21lawsofselflove.com and 30secondtruths.com were inactive, 1pct.io offered a dotted puzzle to users to find rewards for themselves.

Hence, it is anticipated that Testingamazon.com may be offering GIFT CARDS in exchange for some type of purchases or deals, as Leadpages ultimately aims to boost sales.

However, it is uncertain if Testingamazon.com will offer products and services or shut down, as it was registered for only one year and has already passed seven months.

Features of Testingamazon.com

Testingamazon.com is currently acting like a domain parking, without any specific e-store or business claiming its ownership. It did not specify its mission statement, and its URL included the terms ‘testing’ and ‘Amazon.’

The website gained a 62% trust and 62.3% business ranking, although it has a zero Alexa rank, nil Domain Authority, 6% suspicion, 11% malware, 1% spam, and 18% threat and phishing scores.

Reviews of Testingamazon.com

Testingamazon.com has not offered products and services to date, so it cannot be marked as a scam. However, in the past, hundreds of websites have claimed to offer customers Amazon’s products for testing and feedback.

Such websites took personal, contact, and payment details from users and misused them. In some cases, customers were lured into buying products under the banner of testing them.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, Testingamazon.com’s legitimacy is uncertain as it has not commenced operations to date. While it may offer GIFT CARDS in exchange for some type of purchases or deals, it is unclear if it will offer products and services or shut down.

As of now, it cannot be marked as a scam. However, customers should be cautious of such websites that offer products for testing and feedback, as they may misuse personal and payment details.
