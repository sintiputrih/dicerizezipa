Biography:

Stephanie Leonidas was born on February 14, 1984 in London, United Kingdom. She began acting at age eight by performing in community theatre. By the time she was nine, she had obtained an agent, which got her acting gigs on television. She made her debut in an episode of the TV series Chalk in 1997. Her next role came in 2000 with another guest appearance in Down to Earth.

Her first significant role was as Della Wells in the British soap opera Night and Day in 2001. In the next few years, she appeared as a guest star in The Bill, Rose and Maloney, and Revelations. She also appeared in the miniseries Empire. In 2013, she gained recognition for starring as Irisa Nyira on the American series Defiance. As a film actress, she’s known for her role in MirrorMask. Other films are Feast of the Goat and How to Stop Being a Loser.
