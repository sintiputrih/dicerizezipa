Amelia Rose Earhart Biography

Amelia Rose Earhart is an American private pilot and former reporter for NBC affiliate KUSA-TV in Denver, Colorado. She was born on January 18th, 1983 in Downey, California, United States. In 2013 Amelia started the Fly With Amelia Foundation, which grants flight scholarships to girls aged 16–18.

Amelia was told by family members in her youth that she was a descendant of Amelia Mary Earhart. When Amelia was in college, she hired a genealogist to research her connection to Amelia Earhart. That genealogist told her that Amelia and Amelia shared a “distant common ancestry traced back to the 1700s”, however, a second genealogical search in 2013 found there was no traceable connection.

Amelia took her first flying lesson on June 2, 2004, and obtained her private pilot certificate in a Cessna 172. In December 2011, Amelia recreated her namesake’s transcontinental flight from Oakland, California to Miami, Florida in a Cirrus SR22, as a completion of her instrument training hours.
