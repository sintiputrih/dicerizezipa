How many times did Louis Armstrong marry?

Louis Daniel Armstrong, also known by the stage names “Satchmo,” “Satch,” and “Pops,” was an American trumpeter and singer who lived from August 4, 1901, to July 6, 1971.

He was one of the most important people in jazz. His career spans five decades as well as many jazz periods. Numerous honors were bestowed upon him, including the Grammy for Best Male Vocal Performance for Hello, Dolly! in 1965, the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award posthumously given to him in 1972, and his 2017 entry into the National Rhythm & Blues Hall of Fame.

Armstrong grew up in New Orleans where he was born. Armstrong, a creative trumpet and cornet musician who rose to fame in the 1920s, had a fundamental impact on jazz by reorienting the music’s emphasis from group improvisation to solo performance. Around 1922, he traveled to Chicago with his tutor Joe “King” Oliver to join the Creole Jazz Band.
