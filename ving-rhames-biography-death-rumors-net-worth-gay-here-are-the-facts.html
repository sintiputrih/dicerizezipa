Ving Rhames Biography

Ving Rhames was born Irving Rameses Rhames on May12. 1959 in New York City. He attended the New York School of Performing Arts and graduated in 1978. He studied acting at the State University of New York, Purchase. After his freshman year at SUNY, he transferred to the Juilliard School of Drama in New York City on a scholarship to study classical theater.

After graduating from Julliard in 1983, the classically trained actor made his television debut in the 1984 drama Go Tell It On The Mountain . Rhame’s first big break came that same year, after landing a starring role on Broadway ‘s The Winter Boys. He has an extensive list of works ranging from supporting roles to leading roles both on stage and screen. Disregarding his recurring TV roles on shows like Another World, Miami Vice, and Pioneering Light in the 1980s, notable big-screen roles include portraying Jack in the film Native Son(1986) and plays Lieutenant Reilly in the 1989 film War Dead. Ving is best known for his villainous character Marsellus Wallace in the 1994 film Pulp Fiction, starring in Don King: Only in America (1997), and ace computer hacker Luther Stickell in the blockbuster series Mission Impossible. Alongside Tom Cruise, Ving Rhames is one of only two actors to appear in every Mission Impossible film (1996 – 2015).

His outstanding performance in Brian De Palma’s Mission: Impossible brought him more notoriety and fame and paved the way for more roles alongside other Hollywood stars. Rhames has worked on other films such as Con Air (1997), Out of Sight (1998), Entrapment (1999) , the remake of the 1978 zombie film Dawn of the Dead ( 2004), Perfect Match (2007), I’ll Pronounce You Now Chuck and Larry (2007) , A Sunday Horse (2016), Father Figures (2017) and more. Meanwhile, he continued to play important roles on the small screen, including his role as Peter Benton’s brother-in-law on the medical series ER. The man also lent his voice to animated films such as Lilo & Stitch (2002) and The Star (2017) .
